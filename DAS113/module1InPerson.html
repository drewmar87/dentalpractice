Good Day Everyone,

Welcome to our first day of DAS 113, where we'll be sinking our teeth into Tooth Anatomy and Identification! Here's a rundown of what we'll be doing today. We'll kick things off with introductions. I'd love for each of you to share your name and what sparked your interest in dental assisting. Following that, we'll dive into our syllabus and course schedule. You'll get a clear understanding of our course expectations, grading criteria, and what lies ahead in our learning journey. We will also dedicate some time for any questions and feedback you may have. After a short break, we'll immerse ourselves in tooth identification. You'll be learning about dental arches, quadrants, and the types of teeth. We'll also take a sneak peek at the universal numbering system. Our next phase will take us deeper into tooth anatomy. You'll learn about substances like enamel, dentin, cementum, and pulp. We'll follow this up with a group activity to label these components on a tooth. The groups will then share their findings, and we'll rectify any misconceptions. Post another break, we'll explore the five surfaces of a tooth. To reinforce this learning, we'll have an interactive online quiz for immediate formative assessment. We'll round off our class with a recap of today's key learning points and a brief glimpse into our next class. There will be plenty of time for you to ask any questions about today's session or the course in general. Let's kickstart this exciting journey in dental assisting!
